KPU Faculty Scheduler

At KPU, all of the IT students need to complete large-scale projects. One of these project options was to solve the issue of KPU’s scheduling inefficiency that forced faculty to schedule professors by hand each semester which produced a time consuming and tedious process for staff. This assignment was outsourced to the students to see if they could create a reliable in-house project that could be used for the teachers. My group undertook this project to try to solve that problem.

Scheduling Algorithm


Despite this being one of my earlier projects at KPU, this was definitely the most technologically advanced piece of work that I had worked at up until this point. The project called upon a lot of my fundamental programming skills, and I learned quite a bit about recursion and coding architecture. The flow of the program follows a series of steps that the schedule needs to follow which rules out teachers, rooms, and time periods with each one. For each teacher that needs to be scheduled they must have a room that is not booked, a time they are not busy, in a subject they are proficient in and in a campus that they can teach at. As the scheduler runs, it takes these filters and the algorithm goes through it, eliminating what their options are until the schedule can be made. This recursively runs until all teachers and classes have been outfitted with a match. Finally, I implemented a PDF output which allows the user to print the finalized schedule



UI & UX Design


My most recent version of the KPU faculty scheduler was an attempt at redesigning the project to give it a fresh new coat of paint. I found that my original pass of the project was lacking in the UI in UX design. It was very difficult to show others the general workflow of the project as a user and the colour scheme and design lacked quality. I spent a decent amount of time testing and trying to find the right workflow that made sense to somebody who would be scheduling teachers. What I found was that utilizing a left to right experience on data entry helped achieved my project goals a lot easier.
