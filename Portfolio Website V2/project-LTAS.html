Let's Take A Selfie!

Your name is Jimmy, and you are the world's best internet influencer. You risk your life and more importantly, your reputation to achieve the perfect livestream to gain the approval of thousands of internet strangers. You ascend the heights of the bustling and lively Vancouver to accomplish your internet fame.

Will this Icarus "fly" to heights no person has gone before?
Or will he fly too close to the sun and descend to the depths of internet infamy?

Key Features

Let’s Take a Selfie was a 2-day game jam between myself and a couple of friends, who at the time were newer to the game development field. All of my personal goals in developing a good game jam game were met for this project and I consider this game a great success with all of the restrictions we placed on ourselves. Our team was one of the best submissions to the "Van Jam" game jam.


My major roles and tasks done on this project include:

- Worked quickly within a tightly defined timeline to complete design, programming, art and sound
- Learned about programming in Unity’s 2D environment as well as platformer game design
- Developed new mechanics in a fast-paced environment.
- Fabricated the most polished product possible with a small scope and timeline

2D Gameplay


The main goal that I focused on when working on this project to make a fun and fast paced 2D game with a movement system that felt solid and smooth to play while being easy to pick up in a short amount of time. The team managed to produce a unique momentum system with the use of the selfie stick to influence the direction of the character while also creating acceleration-based movement with wall jumping mechanics that incorporated variable jumping heights. All of these mechanics together played very nicely and were all obtainable goals for our timeline.



Parallax Effects


For this project, we wanted to have more visually interesting components to the art while under our time constraints We decided to use the parallax effect to make the visuals more interesting and living. I had seen it done in many games before but never learned how it was achieved. Thus, I made it a goal to research and design this effect so that the visuals of the game felt more fast paced and less static when the character was moving. The effect was achieved with 3 separated layers moving at different speeds including a city, sky and a cloud layer.



Fast Iteration Design


Let’s Take a Selfie was a very fast pace working environment. The team had two days to complete the game and polish it as much as possible to meet our submission deadline. Many of our team members were new at game development which left me as the most experienced member working on the project and was designated as the team leader. I took the role to heart, wanting to ensure that every team member would create work that they could be proud of. Whilst managing my own workload, I tried my best to help others where they needed it. By working on this project, I developed deeper group management skills. I had to understand when it was appropriate to gather a group consensus or which decisions should be made as a personal choice. Finding a perfect balance between the two options ensures that the team can effectively meet the deadline, avoid scope creep and keep team members happy with their influence over the project. I found that this is a great ability for a team player to know where a decision falls between a quick choice and a large impact decision.
