Tera Dragon

A new planet has recently been found and contains crystals that make great power sources. The planet is home to dangerous creatures which have prevented the collection and study of these crystals for some time.

As an aspiring entrepreneur, you decide to give it a shot, after all, you’ll be rich for life if you succeed. It's up to you to explore the planet, dig up these crystals, and prevent the native creatures from stopping you.

Key Features

Tera Dragon was a pretty unique project for me both socially and technologically. I used Reddit’s “I Need A Team” (INAT) forum to find like minded developers who were interested in creating games together to build their portfolios. I have used the subreddit to participate in a couple projects in the past. Each one was unique and interesting, but the platform isn’t without problems. Projects require a strong leader who can inspire motivation and passion within each member in order to keep the team dedicated to a project that they have no contractual obligation towards. Using my previous experience with INAT projects, I started my own group and placed myself as in a dedicated leader role to create Tera Dragon. My main focus was to become a strong team leader who can effectively fill any personnel gaps within the project with my skills while motivating my team to create a finished product.


My major roles and tasks done on this project include:

- Focused on team leadership and recruitment of the team
- Set up project tasks such as timeline scheduling, creating time estimates and creating Trello boards
- Worked with other team members in programming and system design
- Utilized the Unity engine to develop 2D systems and use existing systems such as Unity tile maps
- Designed game mechanics and created play testing documentation

2D Tower Defense


During the formation of the group, we each came up with our own ideas and game designs. Amongst many of the different game ideas that we pitched between each other, we found the idea of a 2D tower defence to be the most agreeable project that would help us avoid scope creep, allow for a scalable project, and allow us to work with new systems. We played with the idea of balancing the player’s defences and resource gathering together within the same currency system. This would make the player maximize their damage output for a later rounds by sacrificing their current power. This idea came with a slew of different systems designs and ideas that we each designed and incorporated together with our team of programmers.



Unity Tilemaps


One of the key features that Unity provides for 2D game developers is the tile map system. Before this project, I had heard amazing things about this system but lacked the experience to use it in my own projects. The implementation of this system was a bit of an uphill battle as I am normally used to developing for 3D games. This was a step in a new direction, but I really wanted to expand my horizons helping me explore different versions on how some mechanics could be implemented. I learned a lot about image compression, pixel perfect sizing and various other tools to make games look better and pop for the player from the 2D perspective.



Remote Leadership


I found being in a leadership and producer role for Tera Dragon one of the most interesting parts about working on this project. From the beginning I took initiative to create recruit posts on reddit and conduct interviews for the team members. I knew that this would be tough as a hobbyist project, rather than waiting for dedicated “unicorn” team members who had the perfect amount of time to work on the project, I decided to gather several members who could work smaller hours on the project. In the end, I think this was for the best as the team was filled with fun and vibrant people contributed to the game in valuable ways. During my time on this project, I had to create steps and documentation on how we were going to achieve our goals to complete the game. I noticed that having a dedicated list of tasks, expectations, and ideas on how to complete them greatly improved the morale and completion rate of the team. It can be quite difficult to motivate the team of a hobbyist project. However, I found that it helps when you appeal to each member’s wants and needs so that everyone can feel a sense of pride when they achieve their goals. A lot of time and effort went into managing the project, but I also had juggle being the producer, handling objectives such as task scheduling, creating mechanic and implementation documents, and art requirements. I also had to fill the role as a programmer too in order to gain clear understanding of how the game works beneath the hood so that I could direct the technical team members with a strong understanding of how the game functions.
