VR Play Space

I have always had an interest in new and advanced technology. This inspired me to get an Oculus development kit that I grew very fond of while a tinkered with it for years. When virtual reality (VR) increased in popularity and consumer headsets were released, I was one of the first people to pre-order a headset, the HTC Vive. As an early adopter, I saw the possible business applications of this technology by showing it off to average consumers, who typically aren’t familiar with VR. Thus, Virtual Reality Place Space was born.

Key Features

I created Virtual Reality Play Space as a mobile virtual reality setup to provide interactive demos to large groups and gatherings. I mainly focused on high school graduation parties. As my first entrepreneurial experience, I would have to say that this was an amazing success. While I might have been naïve and under sold my skills at first, I gained so much from the hard work I put into the entrepreneurial project I created.

My major roles and tasks done on this project include:

Demoed interactive content to users new to VR.
Created business documents and promotional advertising material.
Gained experience in the process of starting a company and managing a business.
Reached out clients with cold calls to attract new business.

Entrepreneurial & Social Skills


My business in virtual reality was not only a big step forward for me professionally but socially as well. I have always been very introverted, yet I thought of this chance as the perfect opportunity for personal growth, strengthening both entrepreneurial and social skills. In the beginning, I felt inexperienced in the business field but was eager to learn. As the business was getting off the ground, I needed to do a lot of cold calling to create opportunities for the business. I had no idea how much work running a business was. However, I worked really hard for my project to thrive, anything from booking appointments to creating demos with new technology and developing a professional business identity.



Demoing For VR


One of the most transferable skills that I learned from this business was figuring out how to demo and showcase technology and games to others. It was a huge mountain to overcome from growing up as a wall flower, but I found as I learned the skills to show and teach others it became easier with each passing day. I learned a lot about how to interact with technology in relation to different age and experience groups, how new players flow with the games that they are presented with as well as the smaller things like where their eyes drift towards in a normal play session or what sound cues I can expect players to acknowledge or dismiss. I found an introductory spiel was just as important as reading players interactions with the media they consumed. This project taught me that it was important to adapt my hosting style as either active or passive to best help each and every player to experience them medium.
